Babycorn Manchurian

Description:

Babycorn Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish featuring crispy baby corn coated in a spicy, tangy, and sweet sauce. It's a vegetarian-friendly appetizer or main course with a perfect balance of flavors.

The dish typically involves deep-frying baby corn pieces, then tossing them in a flavorful sauce made with soy sauce, vinegar, chili sauce, and other seasonings. The result is a crispy, flavorful, and slightly spicy treat.

Ingredients:

  • Babycorn, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Cornflour
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Tomato sauce
  • Green chili sauce
  • Garlic paste
  • Ginger paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Sugar
  • Cornstarch

Steps:

  1. Prepare the babycorn: Mix babycorn pieces with cornflour and a little water.
  2. Deep fry: Deep fry the coated babycorn until golden brown and crispy.
  3. Make the sauce: In a pan, heat oil. Add garlic and ginger paste. Saute for a few seconds.
  4. Combine flavors: Add soy sauce, vinegar, tomato sauce, green chili sauce, salt, and sugar. Stir well.
  5. Thicken the sauce: Mix cornstarch with a little water and add it to the sauce to thicken.
  6. Combine: Add the fried babycorn to the sauce and coat evenly.
